Look father dost thou see that shining star That seems to stand above town so far, and 'T 's like wondrous blossom on a stem also see it ever shines o'er Bethlehem, a brighter ace myself 'm of course I never saw-- also perfect form without a speck or flaw. stranger ace; it never shone before It standeth always above that stable door, look ye I have found little lamb new-born, poor little beast, and wrap him well also warm, and an ill night to be born in frost also snow Naught but cold skies supra cold ground earth below. myself marvel any little creature should be born On such night, myself found it all forlorn Crying beside its mother in the storm, as hark myself thought myself heard sound of mighty wings, and listen; and is it the winter sky this sings; nay gray other I heard naught. You are old also gray And weary with the miles you have walked today. At noon myself met a man who carried of shade He led a mule also riding it maid-- A maiden with a face myself will ne'er forget wondrous face myself seem to understand it yet Lit with an inward shining as if God Had set a lighted lamp within her soul. Many have passed all day long but none such as these And no face have I ever seen like hers, like the man and maid were strangers here And come to Bethlehem at king 's command. Methinks I met that very man and maid-- maiden with such wondrous dove-like eyes I 've seen them near this place all just little tired also worn grudging about town seeking an inn, and and did they find one; yet nay not so, for every inn was crowded to its doors.
