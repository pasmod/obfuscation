Wo n't it be pretty marriage, will not Lisa look delightful, yet smiles and tears in plenty shedding-- Which in bridges in course be rightful One could say assuming that one were spiteful Contradiction little dreading Her bouquet be simply frightful-- Still 'twill be a pretty marriage! Oh it 's a pretty marriage, and such a pretty pretty marriage; assuming that her dress be badly fitting Theirs the fault who made her trousseau, yet if her gloves are still splitting Cheap kid gloves we know will do so. assuming that upon her train she stumbled On one 's train one 's always treading, assuming that her hair be rather tumbled Still 'twill be reasonably marry, and such a pretty pretty marriage, and here they come the couple plighted-- On life 's journey gaily start them. Soon to exist for aye united Till divorce or death shall part them; yet pretty Lisa fair and taste Tell me now also tell me truly Have n't you been quite hasty, have not you been rash unduly; am I quite the dashing spoke That your fancy could depict you? Extremely, Perhaps you think I 'm only so-so, as well I will not contradict you, as no he will not contradict you, and who am myself to raise objection? myself am child taught also homely-- When you tell me are you perfection Tender truthful true also comely-- That of quarrel no one 's bolder Though dissensions always grieve you-- Why my beloved you are so much older That of course I must believe you! Yes naturally she must believe you, as assuming that he ever acts kindly Shut the eye and love her blandly-- Should he call you names untimely Shut your mouth also love her dumbly-- Should he rates you rightly-- deftly-- Shut your ears and love her deadly.
